karmaṇy evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana

“Thy right is to work only, but never with its fruits; let not the fruits of actions be thy motive, nor let thy attachment be to inaction.”

Bhagavad Gita 2.47

In Plain Terms

This is probably the most famous verse in the Gita, and it's famous because it solves about half of your problems in one line. You get to do the work. You don't get to control the results.
